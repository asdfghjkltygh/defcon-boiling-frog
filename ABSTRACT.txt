Boiling Frog: Extracting and Trapping Cloud Auto-Scalers

Cloud auto-scalers, Kubernetes HPAs, and monitoring-driven scaling policies
all share a property their operators don't think about: the decision boundary
between "do nothing" and "scale out" is a deterministic function of filtered
telemetry. If the filter pipeline is deterministic, and in production it
almost always is, that boundary is extractable via binary search.

This tool demonstrates the extraction attack end-to-end. Seven probes map
the scaling threshold of an AWS Auto Scaling Group running SMA, Kalman, or
raw-passthrough filters, with 100% success and zero detection surface. The
extracted boundary is then weaponized into a Denial of Wallet attack:
holding load just above the threshold during off-peak forces phantom
instances that serve no real traffic, projecting to ~$1.01M/mo in
damages at 850 RPS sustained.

The tool also ships a defense: a Differential Privacy Governor that injects
calibrated Laplace noise into the telemetry pipeline, making the boundary
probabilistic. Each attacker probe now independently carries a 17.4%
chance of being absorbed, visible to the SOC, invisible to the attacker.
After five probes, cumulative detection probability exceeds 60%. The
defense adds sub-millisecond latency and produces <0.001% spurious
triggers on stationary traces.

We release the full exploit, the defense, a 200-probe Monte Carlo
evaluation, and a 5-minute interactive demo.
